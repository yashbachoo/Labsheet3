Explore Amazing Animals

Lion (Panthera leo)

Habitat: Grasslands, savannas, open woodlands in Africa; small population in India.

Diet: Carnivore – mostly antelopes, zebras, buffalo.

Behavior: Social, live in prides of 10–40 lions; males defend territory; females do most hunting.

Fun Fact: Lion roars can be heard up to 8 km away.

Elephant (Loxodonta africana / Elephas maximus)

Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and deserts in Africa and Asia.

Diet: Herbivore – grasses, leaves, fruits, bark.

Behavior: Highly social; matriarchal herds; use trunks for feeding, drinking, and communication.

Fun Fact: Elephants have excellent memory and can recognize themselves in mirrors.

Giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis)

Habitat: Savannas and open woodlands in Africa.

Diet: Herbivore – leaves, especially from acacia trees.

Behavior: Generally peaceful; males fight by swinging necks (“necking”).

Fun Fact: Giraffes have 7 neck vertebrae, same as humans, but each vertebra is very long.

Penguin (Spheniscidae)

Habitat: Southern Hemisphere; mostly Antarctica and sub-Antarctic islands.

Diet: Carnivore – fish, krill, squid.

Behavior: Flightless but excellent swimmers; live in colonies; strong parental care.

Fun Fact: Emperor penguins can dive over 500 meters deep for fish.

Giant Panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca)

Habitat: Mountain forests in China.

Diet: Primarily bamboo; occasionally small animals.

Behavior: Mostly solitary; excellent climbers; spend 10–16 hours eating daily.

Fun Fact: Newborn pandas are tiny (~100 g) and blind; adults can weigh up to 100 kg.

Dolphin (Delphinidae)

Habitat: Oceans and rivers worldwide.

Diet: Carnivore – fish, squid, crustaceans.

Behavior: Highly intelligent; live in pods; use echolocation to hunt and communicate.

Fun Fact: Dolphins have unique signature whistles to identify individuals.

Koala (Phascolarctos cinereus)

Habitat: Eucalyptus forests in eastern Australia.

Diet: Herbivore – eucalyptus leaves.

Behavior: Sleep 18–20 hours per day; mostly solitary; carry young in pouches.

Fun Fact: Koalas have fingerprints very similar to humans.

Tiger (Panthera tigris)

Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and mangroves in Asia.

Diet: Carnivore – deer, boar, small mammals.

Behavior: Solitary apex predator; territorial; strong swimmers.

Fun Fact: Stripes are unique like human fingerprints and provide camouflage in forests.
